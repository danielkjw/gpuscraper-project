            <span class="comments-title-content" >Top tier performance at mid tier price. Bring an extra fan though.</span>
        
        <span class="comments-text comments-time comments-time-right" content="2020-05-04"">5/4/2020 9:04:13 AM</span>

<div class="comments-content">

    
            <p>
                <strong>Pros:</strong>
                Phenomenal power for the price. Overclock it and it cranks out 1440p 144hz in most any current game. Cooler and RGB accents look sleek and blend well with most any build aesthetic.
            </p>
        
            <p>
                <strong>Cons:</strong>
                Runs HOT, especially at faster clocks. The cooler keeps up, mind you, and doesn&#39;t even make that much noise doing it. The stock fan profile is noisy, but the card stays at around 90 C &#40;&#42;see note below&#41; in my case whether I max the fans or cap them at 40&#37; duty cycle. At 40&#37; they&#39;re much quieter and I can&#39;t hear them at all through headphones. I&#39;m told you can make it much cooler by running at stock clocks and undervolting, but that&#39;s not what I got this card for.
            </p>
        
    
        <p>
            <strong>Overall Review:</strong>
            You can spend a lot more to get a bit more, but you have to go a long, long way down the performance scale before you beat this chip. This particular card overclocks like a dream and, unless you&#39;re after high framerates at 2160p, you don&#39;t need any more than that for today&#39;s games.<br><br>&#42; Regarding the near boiling temperatures, the whole card doesn&#39;t actually run that hot. To push the performance envelope, AMD went from the old method of taking average die temperature and throttling the entire unit before any one processor core is likely to overheat based on that, to instead measuring the actual temperature of each core and adjusting their individual voltages and frequencies on the fly. The reported &#34;junction&#34; temperature is an average of the &#40;much higher&#41; at-core mesaurements, and because the card will back off on any core that is overheating, the thermal throtting limit on these cards is a blistering 110 degrees C. That said, because the card can push all the cores to the limit instead of just one &#40;with extra margin for error,&#41; the actual thermal output of these cards is high compared to recent generations &#40;although if you remember the old &#34;Thermi&#34; chips, even this card isn&#39;t THAT hot.&#41; So don&#39;t even think about running one of these overclocked without very good case ventilation and a reliable power supply.
